Chapter 3 - Come Away, Come Away!

For a moment after Mr. and Mrs. Darling left the house the night-lights by the beds of the three children continued to burn clearly. 
They were awfully nice little night-lights, and one cannot help wishing that they could have kept awake to see Peter Pan, who was just then drawing near the house.

Flying low over the trees in the garden, he alighted softly on the floor of the nursery. 
The window was open, and he stepped inside. 
His shadow, however, remained outside and began flitting about the walls. 
He tried to fasten it on with soap, as children do with postage-stamps, but it would not stick.
